What is Facial Recognition Technology (FRT)?

Facial Recognition Technology (FRT) is a type of biometric system that uses a person’s unique facial features, like the distance between the eyes, nose, and mouth, to identify or verify their identity. It captures an image of a face, converts it into data, and compares it with images stored in a database.

Why It’s Important

Facial Recognition Technology (FRT) is important because it allows systems to identify or verify individuals using their facial features. It plays a significant role in enhancing security, streamlining identification processes, and enabling contactless verification. As a biometric tool, it provides a faster and often more accurate method of identity confirmation compared to traditional ID checks.

Application Domain

  • Security and Law Enforcement: Surveillance systems in public spaces identify suspects or missing persons.
  • Mobile Devices & Consumer Electronics: Face unlock features enhance user convenience and protection.
  • Airports and Border Control: Automated passport control reduces wait times and improves ID management.
  • Retail and Marketing: Tracks customer behavior and delivers personalized ads.
  • Healthcare: Used for patient identification and access control in hospitals.
  • Banking and Finance: Enables secure online banking and ATM withdrawals.

Why It Matters

The rapidly expanding use of FRT in security, law enforcement, retail, and personal devices raises urgent ethical questions about surveillance, consent, and bias.
