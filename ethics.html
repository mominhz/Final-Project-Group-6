Ethical Discussion on Facial Recognition Technology

Facial Recognition Technology (FRT) presents a range of ethical concerns in its design, implementation, and use. This section examines the key issues from multiple ethical perspectives.

Surveillance & Privacy

FRT can be used for mass surveillance, often without public consent. This raises significant privacy concerns, particularly regarding the collection and storage of biometric data without individuals' knowledge or approval.

Bias & Fairness

Multiple studies show FRT systems may exhibit racial and gender bias, leading to inaccurate identifications and potential discrimination. Ethical deployment requires transparent datasets, unbiased training methods, and continuous testing for fairness.

Responsibility & Accountability

When FRT is misused—by governments, law enforcement, or private companies—who is accountable? Clear policies must define responsibility for harms caused by technical errors, misuse, or overreach.

Consent & Autonomy

Ethical use of FRT demands clear consent from individuals. Technologies that track people without consent violate individual autonomy, a key principle in human rights frameworks.

Conclusion & Recommendations

  • Implement strict privacy regulations for biometric data collection.
  • Mandate bias audits and fairness evaluations before deployment.
  • Develop clear governance models for accountability.
  • Ensure public transparency and offer opt-out mechanisms.
